Early Phase Programs

Neuromyon

With our proprietary plasmid DNA technology (pCK vector) and AAV vector technology, we aim to treat neuromuscular and muscle diseases. Our reengineered vectors are capable of reaching central nervous system or internal organs where it is difficult to reach with plasmid DNA.

Neuromyon program intends to treat neuromuscular diseases based on our proprietary plasmid DNA technology (pCK vector) and AAV vector technology.

Our plasmid DNA technology allows a maximized gene therapy protein expression profile with the possibility of mass production. Its excellent safety profile and protein expression levels can expand its target indications with carefully genes.

Based on technologies and IPs we have secured over the past years, we are building new platforms with even higher expression level and developing next generation plasmid DNA medicines equipped with newly optimized therapeutic genes.

AAV vector is another area of focus in exploring treatment options for intractable diseases, as it can effectively deliver therapeutic genes to body parts such as the central nervous system or internal organs that may be difficult to reach with plasmid DNA.

Chimericsmith

Having expertise in retroviral based ex-vivo gene modification, we are expanding our research to cell mediated gene therapy, especially solid tumor targeting CAR-T cell technology. Currently, we have 4 research-phase pipelines, one being licensed out to Bluebird bio.

Chimericsmith is Helixmith’s Chimeric Antigen Receptor (CAR) program that incorporates years of hands-on experience from ex vivo gene therapy clinical trial involving hematopoietic stem cells as early as a decade ago, through which Helixmith acquired retroviral vector platform and ex vivo gene transfer technology essential to CAR-T therapies.

Helixmith’s first CAR candidate based on its core technology was licensed out to Bluebird bio in 2015. In retroviral vector technology, we hold our own proprietary technology and IPs for retroviral vectors that have high gene expression level and excellent safety profile, with no potential of creating replication-competent retrovirus.

Oncosmith

We are investigating new and innovative approaches to help cure cancer patients, utilizing our plasmid DNA and recombinant protein technology. 2 pipelines are successfully undergoing Phase I and II clinical trials in China and Korea.

Oncosmith is a program that intends to help cancer patients, based on our plasmid DNA and recombinant protein technology.

A therapeutic cancer vaccine program, VM206 targets HER2/neu positive breast or ovarian cancer. HER2/neu is a well-known tumor-associated antigen that is highly expressed in breast, ovarian, and gastric cancer, over-expressed in about 30% of breast cancers and strongly associated with poor prognosis. VM206 can elicit an immune response to HER2/neu, resulting in a reduction in the growth and recurrence rate of breast or ovarian cancer. The Phase I clinical trial of VM206 has been completed in Korea.

A re-engineered form of interleukin 11 (IL-11), VM501 targets chemotherapy-induced thrombocytopenia (CIT). Compared to existing recombinant IL-11 medicine for the disease, VM501 is more effective in low dosage with better safe profile. In Phase I and II clinical trials conducted in China, VM501 showed significant therapeutic effects with without severe adverse effects.
